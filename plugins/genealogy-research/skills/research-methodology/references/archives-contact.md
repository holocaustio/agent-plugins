# Key Archives by Region — Contact & Request Guide

## When to Contact Archives

Contact archives directly when:
- Online databases have an index entry but you need the **original register page** for more detail
- A record type isn't in any online index (e.g., civil marriages for mixed couples, Meldezettel)
- FamilySearch has a film-only record that hasn't been digitized
- You need a certified copy for legal purposes

## How to Write a Request

**Be specific.** Archives handle thousands of requests. Include:
- Full name of the person (with known variants)
- Date or date range (as narrow as possible)
- Type of record (birth, marriage, death, membership)
- Register name if known (e.g., "Geburtsbuch Band XII")
- Entry/Akt number if known (from an index)
- Why you need it (family history research)
- Offer to pay any applicable fees
- If overseas, mention that — some archives accommodate remote requests

**Template:**

```
Subject: Research request — [Person Name], [Record Type], [Year]

Dear [Archive Name],

I am conducting family history research and would like to request a copy of the following record:

Person: [Full Name] (also known as [variants])
Record type: [Birth/Marriage/Death register]
Approximate date: [Date or range]
Register reference: [If known — e.g., "Geburtsbuch, entry around March 1884"]
Entry number: [If known from an index]

I would be grateful for a scan or photocopy of the original register page. I am happy to pay any applicable fees.

Thank you for your assistance.
[Your name]
```

## Archives by Region

### Vienna

| Archive | Email | What They Have | Notes |
|---------|-------|---------------|-------|
| **IKG Wien Matrikenamt** | matriken@ikg-wien.at | Jewish Geburtsbuch, Trauungsbuch, Sterbebuch originals | Contact: DSA Irma Wulz, BA. The primary source for Vienna Jewish vital records. Originals have more detail than JewishGen indexes. |
| **IKG Wien Archiv** | archiv@ikg-wien.at | Community membership records, IKG-Austritte (departures), tax rolls, communal correspondence | Appointment required for in-person. Written request for remote. |
| **WStLA** (Wiener Stadt- und Landesarchiv) | post@ma08.wien.gv.at | Meldezettel (residence cards), Notzivilehe (civil marriages for mixed couples at Bezirksgericht), guardianship records, Heimatrecht files | Meldezettel are gold — show every address, household members, dates. Civil marriages (Notzivilehe) for interfaith couples are here, not at synagogues or churches. |
| **Österreichisches Staatsarchiv** | — (use online request form) | Military records, citizenship, government files | Search at archivinformationssystem.at. Most require written request or on-site visit. |

**Vienna-specific note:** Geburtsanzeigen (birth announcements) are a SEPARATE register from the Geburtsbuch — both may exist for the same person, with different details. The Geburtsanzeige is the initial notification; the Geburtsbuch is the formal register entry.

## Tips for Archive Requests

- **Response times vary wildly** — from same-day to months. Follow up after 2-3 weeks if no response.
- **Fees are common** — most archives charge per scan/page. Typical range: €5-20 per document. Always offer to pay.
- **Language** — write in the archive's language when possible (German for Austrian archives, Czech for Czech, etc.). English is generally accepted but local language gets faster responses.
- **IKG Wien** is particularly responsive for Jewish vital record requests — they handle many genealogy inquiries.
- **WStLA Meldezettel** can take a few weeks but they're thorough — they'll search for all variant spellings.
- **Some archives require proof of relationship** for records less than 100 years old (privacy laws). Explain your family connection.
